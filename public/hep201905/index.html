Sydney Meeting

Half-day Sydney particle physics and cosmology meeting

The Sydney particle physics and cosmology meeting will take place in room SNH3003 in the Sydney Nanoscience Hub at the University of Sydney on Friday 3 May 2019. It is organised by the particle physics group at the University of Sydney and the particle physics and cosmology group at UNSW Sydney. See the indico page for details.

Timetable

Session 1: 13:00-15:00

  • 13:00 Welcome
  • 13:05 Céline Boehm (USyd)
  • 13:20 Belle - Kevin Varvell/Chia-Ling Hsu (USyd) Studying leptonic and semileptonic B decays at the Belle II experiment (pdf)
  • 13:35 Belle - Bruce Yabsley/Frank Meier/Priyanka Cheema (USyd) Belle II calorimetry and analysis R&D (pdf)
  • 13:50 Joe Chen (UNSW) Cosmological N-Body Simulations (pptx)
  • 14:05 Cyril Lagger (USyd) Gravitational Waves from Cosmological Phase Transitions (pdf)
  • 14:20 Yuqi Kang (UNSW) Using Minkowski Functionals to analyse Weak Lensing Maps (pptx)
  • 14:35 Suntharan Arunasalam (USyd) The electroweak phase transition in the standard model with hidden scale invariance (pdf)
  • 14:50 discussion

Break: 15:00-15:15

Session 2: 15:15-17:00

  • 15:15 ATLAS - Carl Suster (USyd) ATLAS activities at Sydney (pdf)
  • 15:30 Jack Bennett (UNSW) Why Neff ≠3.046? (pdf) (pptx)
  • 15:45 Daniel Collison (USyd) Magnetic monopoles in the Standard Model and implications for electroweak baryogenesis (pdf)
  • 16:00 short introductions
  • 16:15 discussion

Pub: 17:00- Nag's Head Hotel
